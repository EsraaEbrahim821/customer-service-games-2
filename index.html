The Perfect Experience Mini-Game

Guide one customer through a full chat journey. Every response changes what the customer remembers.

Mission Brief

You are handling a customer whose internet is down again for the third time this week. The customer works from home and is already frustrated. Your job is to protect trust, reduce effort, and keep the customer guided through the journey.

Your Goal: Build Trust. Calm Emotion. Reduce Effort.

How To Play

  1. Read the customer situation.
  2. Choose the best response.
  3. Watch how your decision affects Trust, Emotion, and Effort.
  4. Complete the journey.
  5. Review your final customer memory.

How Scoring Works

Trust — Did the response build confidence?
Emotion — Did the response calm or escalate the customer?
Effort — Did the response make the customer work harder or easier?
Higher Trust and Emotion are better. Lower Effort is better.
Each of the 5 main decisions also earns points:
Strong = 3 pts  |  Partial = 1 pt  |  Risky or Critical = 0 pts
Total Experience Score = 15 points

Facilitator Debrief Mode

Trainer Discussion Points

  1. The longest response is not always the strongest response.
  2. Empathy without action is incomplete.
  3. Action without emotional acknowledgment can feel cold.
  4. Overpromising creates future trust damage.
  5. Asking too many questions increases effort.
  6. A strong response combines care, clarity, ownership, and next step.
  7. A perfect experience does not mean immediate resolution; it means the customer feels guided and not left alone.

Debrief Questions

  1. Which stage was your most important moment of truth?
  2. Where could trust have been lost quickly?
  3. Which response reduced customer effort the most?
  4. What made the customer feel guided?
  5. Where did you avoid a generic response?
  6. What would the customer remember from your journey?
  7. What habit will you apply in your next real chat?
